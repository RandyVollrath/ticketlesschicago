**FREEDOM OF INFORMATION ACT REQUEST**

To: Chicago Department of Administrative Hearings
Date: [TODAY'S DATE]

RE: Request for Administrative Hearing Outcome Records under the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140/)

Dear FOIA Officer:

Pursuant to the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140/), I hereby request access to the following public records:

**RECORDS REQUESTED:**

All administrative hearing outcome records for parking violations, traffic violations, and automated camera enforcement violations for the period of January 1, 2019 through present, including:

1. **Case Identification:**
   - Ticket number (citation number)
   - Case number
   - Violation code
   - Violation description
   - License plate number (if public record)

2. **Violation Details:**
   - Date of violation
   - Time of violation
   - Location of violation (street address or intersection)
   - Ward number
   - Fine amount (original)

3. **Hearing Information:**
   - Hearing date
   - Hearing type (in-person, written, virtual)
   - Hearing officer ID or name (if public record)
   - Hearing location/facility

4. **Contest Information:**
   - Contest grounds raised by respondent (written arguments/claims)
   - Evidence type submitted (categorized as: photographs, witness statements, documentation, video, dashcam footage, expert testimony, other)
   - Evidence description (if available)
   - Whether respondent appeared in person
   - Whether respondent was represented by counsel

5. **Outcome:**
   - Decision (Liable/Not Liable/Dismissed/Reduced)
   - Reason for decision (if recorded)
   - Final fine amount (if reduced)
   - Whether decision was appealed
   - Appeal outcome (if applicable)

**FORMAT REQUESTED:**

Please provide the data in electronic format (CSV, Excel, or SQL database dump preferred) with the following structure:

```
ticket_number, case_number, violation_code, violation_description,
violation_date, violation_time, location, ward, original_fine,
hearing_date, hearing_type, hearing_officer_id,
contest_grounds, evidence_submitted, evidence_description,
outcome, decision_reason, final_fine, appeal_status
```

**PURPOSE:**

This data will be used for public interest research to:
- Analyze administrative hearing outcomes and success rates by violation type
- Identify which contest grounds and evidence types are most effective
- Provide transparency into the administrative hearing process
- Help Chicago residents understand their rights and make informed decisions about contesting violations
- Improve public access to justice

**FEE WAIVER REQUEST:**

Pursuant to 5 ILCS 140/6(c), I request a waiver of all fees associated with this request on the grounds that:

1. This request is not for a commercial purpose (5 ILCS 140/2(c-10))
2. The disclosure is in the public interest (5 ILCS 140/6(c))
3. The information will be used to benefit the public by providing transparency into administrative hearing processes and outcomes
4. The requested information will help Chicago residents exercise their rights to contest violations

If a fee waiver is denied, please provide an itemized estimate of costs before proceeding.

**LEGAL AUTHORITY:**

- Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140/)
- Administrative hearing outcomes are public records under Illinois law
- The Illinois Supreme Court has held that documents related to administrative proceedings are subject to FOIA disclosure

**RESPONSE TIMELINE:**

Per 5 ILCS 140/3(d), I expect a response within 5 business days. If an extension is needed, please notify me in writing with the reasons and expected completion date.

**RECORDS REQUESTED:**

All records related to red light camera systems operated by the City of Chicago for the period of January 1, 2021 through present, including:

1. **Camera Identification and Location:**
   - Camera ID/serial number
   - Intersection location (street address or cross streets)
   - Installation date
   - Vendor/manufacturer name

2. **Calibration Records:**
   - Calibration date and time
   - Calibration test results (pass/fail)
   - Calibration certificate or report
   - Name of technician or company performing calibration
   - Calibration standards used
   - Any failed calibration tests and dates
   - Corrective actions taken after failed calibration

3. **Maintenance Records:**
   - Maintenance date and type of service
   - Repair records (including reason for repair)
   - Equipment replacement records
   - Downtime periods (when camera was non-operational)
   - Service provider information

4. **Yellow Light Timing:**
   - Yellow light duration (in seconds) for each intersection
   - Speed limit at intersection
   - Date of last timing audit
   - Any changes to yellow light timing (with dates and reasons)
   - Engineering studies justifying timing settings

5. **Technical Issues:**
   - Malfunction reports
   - Complaint logs related to camera accuracy
   - Investigation records of camera problems
   - Any cameras removed or relocated (with dates and reasons)

**FORMAT REQUESTED:**

Please provide in electronic format (CSV or Excel) with structure:
```
camera_id, intersection, installation_date, vendor,
calibration_date, pass_fail, issues_found, resolution,
yellow_light_seconds, speed_limit, last_timing_audit
```

**PURPOSE:**

This information will be used for public interest research to:
- Verify compliance with Illinois automated traffic law enforcement requirements (625 ILCS 5/11-208.6)
- Analyze camera system reliability and accuracy
- Ensure yellow light timing meets federal standards (3.0-4.0 seconds minimum)
- Provide transparency in automated enforcement
- Help Chicago residents understand the reliability of camera evidence

**FEE WAIVER REQUEST:**

Pursuant to 5 ILCS 140/6(c), I request a waiver of all fees on the grounds that disclosure is in the public interest and will benefit the public by ensuring transparency and accountability in automated traffic enforcement.

**LEGAL AUTHORITY:**

- Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140/)
- Illinois Vehicle Code provisions on automated enforcement (625 ILCS 5/11-208.6)

### How to Submit:

**Option 1: Online (Recommended)**
1. Go to: https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/dol/provdrs/foia.html
2. Click "Submit a FOIA Request"
3. Fill out the form
4. Copy/paste the appropriate request text above
5. Submit

**Option 2: Email**
Send to the appropriate department email:
- **Administrative Hearings:** Check CDOAH website for FOIA email
- **Transportation:** CDOT.FOIA@cityofchicago.org
- **General:** foia@cityofchicago.org

**Option 3: Mail**

### What to Expect:

**Timeline:**
- 5 business days: Initial response required
- May extend 5 more days if needed
- Large requests: 2-4 weeks typical

- First 50 pages: Free
- Electronic records: Usually free
- Large database: May claim "commercial purpose" fee
- **Response:** Reiterate public interest + fee waiver request
- **Alternative:** Offer to accept a sample dataset first
